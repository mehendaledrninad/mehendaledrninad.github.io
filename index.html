Dr. Ninad Dileep Mehendale is an Associate Professor in the Electronics Department at K. J. Somaiya College of Engineering and a postdoctoral researcher trained at the prestigious Karlsruhe Institute of Technology, Germany. He holds a Ph.D. in Microfabrication and Image Processing from the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay. His multidisciplinary expertise spans sound signal processing, machine vision, embedded systems, microfluidics and microfabrication, robotics, deep learning, and AR/VR technologies.

Dr. Mehendale has been consistently recognized for his outstanding research contributions and has been listed among the top 2% scientists globally (Stanford–Elsevier ranking) for consecutive years, reflecting his high-impact scientific output. During his academic journey, he also received a gold medal during his M.Tech at NMIMS University and the prestigious Ratan Tata Scholarship for securing the highest marks in the MSBTE diploma examinations.

Beyond teaching and research, Dr. Mehendale actively collaborates with industries and research institutions worldwide. He consults for organizations such as STEMI (Europe), Xenium Digital (India), and the Lofti Zade Center of Excellence, and leads several cutting-edge projects in autonomous systems and artificial intelligence. His innovations include the development of a microfluidic “Rapid Device,” which was the only finalist from India at the international MicroTAS 2017 competition in the United States.

Over recent years, he has authored numerous SCI- and Scopus-indexed publications, contributed to interdisciplinary research in agriculture and IoT-enabled systems, and received grants, including support from Mumbai University for developing an intelligent “robotic ear system” for emergency-response vehicles. Dr. Mehendale continues to drive impactful, application-oriented research at the intersection of electronics, automation, and intelligent systems.
