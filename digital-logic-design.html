01 Course Objectives

Digital systems have a prominent role in everyday life. To understand the operation of digital systems, it is necessary to have a basic knowledge of digital circuits and their logical functions.

The objective of this course is to familiarize the student with fundamental principles of digital design. It has been designed to address multidisciplinary students.

02 Learning Outcomes

CO1

CO2

Mapping Justification

CO1: Number Systems & Codes

  • PO1 (3): Directly applies mathematical knowledge and engineering fundamentals in number systems, conversions, and binary arithmetic operations.
  • PO2 (1): Minimal problem analysis - primarily focuses on applying standard procedures for conversions and arithmetic.
  • PO5 (2): Uses computational tools and techniques for number system conversions and error detection codes.
  • PO11 (2): Establishes foundation for learning advanced digital concepts and emerging technologies.

CO2: Logic Functions

  • PO1 (3): Applies Boolean algebra mathematics and engineering fundamentals to represent and minimize logic functions.
  • PO2 (2): Involves analyzing logic problems, formulating Boolean expressions, and minimizing using K-Map techniques.
  • PO3 (2): Designs optimized logic solutions using different minimization techniques and gate realizations.
  • PO4 (1): Limited investigation through comparison of different minimization approaches.
  • PO5 (3): Extensively uses modern tools like K-Map, Boolean algebra, and circuit simulation for logic minimization.
  • PO11 (2): Develops critical thinking for logic optimization applicable to emerging digital design methodologies.

CO3: Combinational Circuits

  • PO1 (3): Applies comprehensive engineering knowledge to implement arithmetic circuits, multiplexers, encoders, decoders, and comparators.
  • PO2 (2): Analyzes complex combinational problems and selects appropriate MSI devices for implementation.
  • PO3 (3): Designs complete combinational systems using basic gates and MSI devices to meet specific functional requirements.
  • PO4 (2): Investigates circuit behavior through design, testing, and verification of combinational logic.
  • PO5 (3): Utilizes simulation tools, MSI device datasheets, and modern design techniques extensively.
  • PO6 (1): Basic awareness of resource optimization and efficient circuit design for sustainability.
  • PO8 (1): Laboratory work involves collaborative implementation and testing of circuits.
  • PO11 (2): Foundation for advanced combinational design in modern digital systems and FPGAs.

CO4: Sequential Circuits

  • PO1 (3): Applies advanced digital engineering concepts including state machines, flip-flops, counters, and registers.
  • PO2 (2): Analyzes timing diagrams, state transitions, and sequential behavior for complex problems.
  • PO3 (3): Designs complete sequential systems (counters, shift registers) using flip-flops and MSI devices.
  • PO4 (2): Investigates sequential circuit operation through timing analysis and state diagram verification.
  • PO5 (3): Uses timing analyzers, state diagram tools, and MSI sequential devices for implementation.
  • PO6 (1): Understanding of power consumption and timing constraints in sequential designs.
  • PO8 (1): Collaborative laboratory work for building and testing sequential circuits.
  • PO11 (2): Essential foundation for modern synchronous design and microprocessor architecture.

CO5: Advanced Design Foundations

  • PO1 (3): Applies theoretical knowledge of finite state machines and programmable logic devices.
  • PO2 (2): Analyzes state machine behavior and programmable logic implementation strategies.
  • PO3 (2): Designs basic Mealy/Moore machines and understands PROM/PLA/PAL-based implementations.
  • PO4 (1): Limited investigation through state diagram analysis and PLD configuration.
  • PO5 (2): Introduction to programmable logic tools and state machine design software.
  • PO6 (1): Awareness of reconfigurable hardware benefits for sustainable design.
  • PO11 (3): Strong foundation for lifelong learning in FPGA, ASIC design, and advanced digital systems - prepares for emerging technologies.

04 Course Modules

Module 01 9 Hours

Number Representation and Codes

1.1 Number Systems and Conversions: Binary, Octal, Decimal, Hexadecimal, Conversions between number systems, Signed numbers: Two's complement, Sign magnitude
1.2 Binary Operations: Binary Addition and Subtraction (1's and 2's complement method)
1.3 Codes: Binary-coded decimal (BCD), Gray code, Concept of parity for error detection
Module 02 10 Hours

Logic Representation and Minimization Techniques

2.1 Logic Gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR. Universal Gates: Functionality of NAND and NOR. Rules and properties of Boolean algebra
2.2 Minimization: Combinational logic representation using truth table, sum of products (SOP) and products of sum (POS), Minimization techniques using Boolean Algebra, K-Map (2,3,4 Variable), Realization of circuits using Basic gates and Universal gates
Self-Learning Quine-McCluskey method for reduction of SOP/POS expression
Module 03 12 Hours

Combinational Circuits

3.1 Arithmetic Circuits and MSI Devices: Study of combinational circuits such as Adder, subtractor, controlled adder/subtractor, Use of MSI devices for adder. Multiplexer, demultiplexer, encoder, priority encoder, decoder, multiplexer tree and decoder tree. Use of MSI devices for multiplexer, decoder
3.2 Comparators: 1-bit and 2-bit magnitude comparator, 4 bit comparator
Self-Learning BCD adder/subtractor, Binary to 7 segment decoder
Module 04 10 Hours

Sequential Logic Circuits

4.1 Flip-Flops: Latches and flip-flops: Types: SR, D, JK, T flip-flops truth tables, Excitation table, and characteristics equations
4.2 Counters: Asynchronous counters, Synchronous counters, up/down counter, mod counters using flipflops, Use of MSI devices for counters
4.3 Registers: Shift registers using D-FF and types of shift registers (SISO, SIPO, PIPO, PISO). MSI shift register, Ring and Johnson Counter using Shift register
Self-Learning Conversion of Flip-flop
Module 05 4 Hours

Foundations of Digital Design

5.1 Synchronous Machines: Introduction to Synchronous Machine (Mealy and Moore): Block diagram level and its representation as state diagram
5.2 Programmable Logic: Introduction, realization of logic expressions with PROM, PLA and PAL and their implementation
