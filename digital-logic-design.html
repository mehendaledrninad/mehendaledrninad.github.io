01 Course Objectives

Digital systems have a prominent role in everyday life. To understand the operation of digital systems, it is necessary to have a basic knowledge of digital circuits and their logical functions.

The objective of this course is to familiarize the student with fundamental principles of digital design. It has been designed to address multidisciplinary students.

02 Learning Outcomes

CO1

Number Systems & Codes

Understand the fundamentals of number systems, codes, and arithmetic operations.

CO2

Logic Functions

Represent, implement, and minimize logic functions using different techniques.

CO3

Combinational Circuits

Implement combinational logic circuits and their applications using basic gates and MSI devices.

CO4

Sequential Circuits

Implement sequential logic circuits and applications using MSI devices.

CO5

Advanced Design

Understand basic principles required for advanced Digital Design.

03 Course Modules

Module 01 9 Hours

Number Representation and Codes

1.1 Number Systems and Conversions: Binary, Octal, Decimal, Hexadecimal, Conversions between number systems, Signed numbers: Two's complement, Sign magnitude
1.2 Binary Operations: Binary Addition and Subtraction (1's and 2's complement method)
1.3 Codes: Binary-coded decimal (BCD), Gray code, Concept of parity for error detection
Module 02 10 Hours

Logic Representation and Minimization Techniques

2.1 Logic Gates: AND, OR, NOT, NAND, NOR, XOR, XNOR. Universal Gates: Functionality of NAND and NOR. Rules and properties of Boolean algebra
2.2 Minimization: Combinational logic representation using truth table, sum of products (SOP) and products of sum (POS), Minimization techniques using Boolean Algebra, K-Map (2,3,4 Variable), Realization of circuits using Basic gates and Universal gates
Self-Learning Quine-McCluskey method for reduction of SOP/POS expression
Module 03 12 Hours

Combinational Circuits

3.1 Arithmetic Circuits and MSI Devices: Study of combinational circuits such as Adder, subtractor, controlled adder/subtractor, Use of MSI devices for adder. Multiplexer, demultiplexer, encoder, priority encoder, decoder, multiplexer tree and decoder tree. Use of MSI devices for multiplexer, decoder
3.2 Comparators: 1-bit and 2-bit magnitude comparator, 4 bit comparator
Self-Learning BCD adder/subtractor, Binary to 7 segment decoder
Module 04 10 Hours

Sequential Logic Circuits

4.1 Flip-Flops: Latches and flip-flops: Types: SR, D, JK, T flip-flops truth tables, Excitation table, and characteristics equations
4.2 Counters: Asynchronous counters, Synchronous counters, up/down counter, mod counters using flipflops, Use of MSI devices for counters
4.3 Registers: Shift registers using D-FF and types of shift registers (SISO, SIPO, PIPO, PISO). MSI shift register, Ring and Johnson Counter using Shift register
Self-Learning Conversion of Flip-flop
Module 05 4 Hours

Foundations of Digital Design

5.1 Synchronous Machines: Introduction to Synchronous Machine (Mealy and Moore): Block diagram level and its representation as state diagram
5.2 Programmable Logic: Introduction, realization of logic expressions with PROM, PLA and PAL and their implementation
