Course Overview

Digital systems play a crucial role in modern electronics. This course introduces fundamental concepts of number systems, logic design, combinational circuits, sequential circuits, and foundations of digital system design.

Course Outcomes

  • Understand number systems and arithmetic operations
  • Minimize and implement logic functions
  • Design combinational circuits
  • Design sequential circuits
  • Understand advanced digital design foundations

Syllabus

Module 1 – Number Representation and Codes

Binary, Octal, Decimal, Hexadecimal systems, conversions, complements, BCD, Gray code, parity.

Module 2 – Logic Representation and Minimization

Logic gates, Boolean algebra, SOP, POS, K-Map minimization, circuit realization.

Module 3 – Combinational Circuits

Adders, subtractors, multiplexers, encoders, decoders, comparators.

Module 4 – Sequential Logic Circuits

Flip-flops, counters, registers, shift registers, ring and Johnson counters.

Module 5 – Foundations of Digital Design

Mealy and Moore machines, PROM, PLA, PAL implementation.
